Our Story

A year ago, we came across an article regarding children dying in ERs due to staff being unprepared to treat younger patients. This drove us to imagine a solution to both encourage hospitals to gather tools and information necessary to minimize child deaths in the operating room as well as inform parents about which hospitals have emergency rooms ready to take their children. To bring this idea to life, we brought together a team and designed a website to rank ERs based on their ability to treat young patients, because we understand the feeling of concern when a hospital does not meet the requirements of standard care all too well.

Our Mission

We are dedicated to helping kids nationally get the treatment they need. As a team, we analyzed data from various hospitals' emergency departments to understand what was causing these death rates. We came to the conclusion that hospitals were unprepared for patients under the age of 18. This led us to measure pediatric readiness using a set of criteria created with data self-reported by hospitals, which we input into a website to rank how prepared operating rooms are for these patients. We hope that this will help us reach our goal to save the lives of children all over the United States through a user-friendly website.
